Our Group Leaders

  • Our group was founded in 2018 by Mike, hard-wearing father-of-4 and Julia, mother of 3. The difficulties they encountered raising teenage children inspired them to reach out to other parents, share and eventually embrace these problems and help other parents overcome trying situations and problems.

  • Julia and Mike have become qualified therapists since. The group has been growing ever since, and has been involving 2 mental health professionals as well, Brian and Karina.

  • Brian brings 10 years clinical experience as a Counsellor & Psychotherapist for Children and Teenagers.

  • Karina holds a Masters Degree in Health Promotion and a BA (Hons) Degree in Social Care. She worked in the area of homelessness before moving into youth work to operate an internationally recognised youth mentoring programme.

Group Therapy Benefits

Family life is the main factor in our quality of life. If things go wrong in this area, our whole life balance is owerthrown.
Group therapy can help parents work toward and meet many types of goals, like understanding their child better, developing better communication or knowing when it is time to ask for professional intervention.
Sometimes, group therapy is a good fit for that individual or better for treating their concern as they see that they are not on their own. Therapy groups can help parents who find it difficult to get through daily life. Others may not have pressing concerns but still wish for support. They can also benefit from group therapy.

Meetups

Meetups are held twice a week in the sports hall of the Educate Together Secondary School in Limerick, at 18:00 pm on Tuesdays and Thursdays. Premises are the old Salesian secondary school on the South Circular Road (across St. Nessan's Secondary School.) There is plenty of parking available in the school yard and around.
Please make sure that you book your session 2 days in advance. The sessions are free, small donations for the school are welcome, though, as they are providing us the sports hall for free.
Please note that in order to be able to help you efficiently, we have a limit of 15 participants at the time.
